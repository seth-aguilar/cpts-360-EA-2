Activity description: what were the expectations and what did you do?
-I was expected to take over a test system of SEL IEDs and maintain/create communications connections, use SELogic to create tripping conditions and event reporting, and automate the collection of SOE and oscillography data.

Technical decisions: what decisions did you take from a technical perspective throughout this activity/event?
-There were many technical decisions I had to make in my time maintaining this system. These include what type of connection (serial/ethernet), balancing timer logic for tripping and event reporting, and configuration of collection plans through parent devices higher in the device architecture.

Contributions: did you participate alone or with a team? If with a team, what were your specific individual contributions?
-I worked along with a team of 3 high school interns. They would report to me for the most part, unless they had a question beyond my own technical ability. Working in a team like this allowed us to scale out any decisions and work more efficiently.

Quality assessment: how would you assess your participation in this event? What would you do differently if you could redo the event?
-I would say I made meaningful contributions that led to the completion and stability of the test system. If I were to redo this task, I would focus more time on cable management during the early stages of the project in order to prevent it from being an issue later on.
